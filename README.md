# Trading-Battle-Simulator 

A Python-based simulator designed to backtest and compare the performance of different trading strategies against historical stock market data.

##  Overview
This project simulates a "battle" between various algorithmic trading strategies to see which one yields the highest portfolio value over a specific timeframe. It handles data fetching, trade execution logic, and performance visualization.

## Strategies Compared
* **Momentum ($w=20$):** Buys when the price trend is moving upward over a 20-day window.
* **Mean Reversion ($w=20, k=1.5$):** Operates on the theory that prices return to their average, using a 1.5 standard deviation threshold.
* **Random (Baseline):** A control strategy that trades randomly to provide a benchmark for "luck" vs. logic.

**Key Observations:**
* **Momentum** was the dominant strategy for this period, successfully riding the long-term growth of Apple.
* **Mean Reversion** struggled during strong trending periods, as it often bet against the move too early.
* The **Random** baseline provides a clear visual of the "alpha" (excess return) generated by the Momentum strategy.
